User Journey Map

This visual map shows the key stages, actions, and experience flow of users interacting with the AR museum system.

In the Museum Tales system, the user journey maps illustrate the interactive experiences and emotional fluctuations of two representative visitor types. Emily Zhang represents the primary user group—university students and frequent museum visitors—who seek an immersive, highly interactive, and visually engaging AR exhibition experience. Her journey is divided into five stages:

  1. Arrive & Discover: Users obtain information at the museum entrance, exhibition posters, or via the mobile app. First-time encounters may cause some uncertainty about how to start, but clear visual cues and concise onboarding information gradually build confidence, with a slight increase in positive emotions.
  2. Explore & Scan: Users scan exhibit labels with their phones to unlock story fragments. If scan points are not obvious or content is unclear, users may experience temporary confusion and a minor dip in emotions; these issues can be mitigated with hints and short visual explanations.
  3. Interact & Play: Users engage with virtual characters, complete missions or tasks, and make choices. As tasks are successfully completed, emotions rise significantly, and the experience becomes enjoyable and immersive.
  4. Track & Progress: Users monitor their progress via dashboards, unlock new chapters, and collect rewards. If progress feedback is unclear or rewards are insufficient, emotions may slightly drop; using visual progress tracking and meaningful reward mechanisms can improve the experience.
  5. Share & Reflect: Users share their discoveries and review their experience through social media or in-app galleries. Smooth sharing features and achievement displays help restore positive emotions, concluding the journey on a happy and satisfied note.

The lower half of the map shows the journey of Mr. David Lee, representing the secondary user group—parents and occasional museum visitors—who prioritize a simple, clear, and family-friendly experience. His journey is also divided into five stages:

  1. Arrive & Get Started: Users obtain initial information via entrance signage, staff guidance, or instruction boards, and launch the AR system using simple guides or apps. For tech-inexperienced parents, excessive information or small text may cause confusion and slightly lower emotions, but clear signage and readable instructions help boost confidence.
  2. Follow & Scan: Users scan exhibits with labels, AR scan guides, and help buttons. Children may struggle to aim the camera or operate the system, causing brief frustration and a slight dip in emotions; intuitive scanning cues and step-by-step guidance allow parents and children to quickly gain confidence and restore positive experience.
  3. Learn & Explore: Users watch AR content, narration/captions, and perform simple interactions. If children struggle with controls or content pace is too fast, they may lose interest, further lowering emotions; large fonts, audio explanations, and family-friendly interactive activities can improve engagement and elevate mood.
  4. Stay on Track: Users follow progress guides, next-step indicators, and support features to monitor and complete tasks. Ambiguous steps or too much information may confuse parents, slightly lowering emotions; clear next-step instructions and fewer options increase control and improve emotional experience.
  5. Finish & Review: Users review learning outcomes via summary screens, family galleries, and sharing features. Family-friendly review and sharing mechanisms allow parents and children to reflect together and preserve memories, significantly boosting emotions and concluding the AR experience with satisfaction and enjoyment.

Overall, these two user journey maps not only illustrate the behavioral paths, pain points, and improvement opportunities for different visitor types, but also clearly depict emotional fluctuations throughout the experience. This insight helps the design team optimize each touchpoint, enabling Museum Tales to deliver a fully immersive, engaging, and enjoyable AR museum experience.
