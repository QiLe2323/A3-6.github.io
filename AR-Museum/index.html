My Stories in Museums

An immersive AR storytelling journey layered over the museum experience.

Museums are often designed as spaces for observation rather than participation. Visitors typically move from one exhibit to another, reading labels and passively receiving information. However, this traditional model overlooks a crucial aspect of museums: they are not only repositories of objects, but also carriers of human stories, emotions, and cultural exchanges.

Our project, My Stories in Museums, aims to transform museum experiences from passive viewing into active storytelling. Inspired by the exhibition narrative structure in our script , we noticed that each artifact is embedded in multiple perspectives—archivists, exchange students, guards, and visitors all interpret the same object differently. This multiplicity of voices suggests that meaning in museums is not fixed, but co-constructed.

◫ Museum

We chose to explore this theme through a role-playing and interactive system because it allows users to “enter” the museum narrative rather than just observe it. By combining AR interaction, identity-based gameplay, and fragment collection mechanics, users are encouraged to engage with exhibits, converse with virtual characters, and ultimately curate their own micro-exhibition.

This project reflects our belief that museums should not only display history, but also invite visitors to become storytellers themselves. Project（click it）
