Target Users

Museum Visitors Seeking Interactive Experiences

Our target users are museum visitors who seek a more engaging and interactive experience, including:

General visitors who are interested in cultural and historical content but lack deeper understanding
Visitors who tend to lose attention during traditional exhibition viewing
Users who prefer immersive experiences rather than passive information reading
Individuals or groups visiting together who expect shared or social interaction

Current Problems

These users commonly face the following problems in current museum experiences:

Low engagement: Exhibits are primarily static and text-based, leading to passive information consumption
Lack of immersion: Users find it difficult to enter historical contexts or feel “present” in the narrative
Limited interactivity: There are few opportunities for hands-on exploration or meaningful interaction
Fragmented experience: The visit lacks a coherent storyline or goal-driven progression
Weak social interaction: There are limited mechanisms for collaboration or interaction between visitors

System Needs

Therefore, the system needs to support:

Transforming users from passive viewers into active participants
Providing immersive and context-rich experiences
Creating a coherent narrative or task-driven progression
Enabling interaction and collaboration between users
Delivering information in a more intuitive and engaging way
