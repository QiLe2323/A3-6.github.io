Essential Features for a Playful Museum System

To ensure the system is truly “playful”, it must include the following essential features:

1. Role-playing Interaction (Must-have)

Users must be able to take on the role of a historical character and experience the museum from that perspective.
→ This transforms passive viewing into active participation.

2. AR-based Exploration (Must-have)

The system must support AR interactions with real exhibits, such as:
Scanning artifacts to unlock story fragments
Triggering virtual characters or scenes

→ This creates a bridge between physical objects and digital narratives.

3. Game Progression Mechanism (Must-have)

The experience must include clear progression, such as:
Tasks or missions
Unlocking new stages
Story advancement

→ This maintains engagement and gives users a sense of achievement.

4. Social Interaction (Must-have)

Users must be able to interact with other players, for example:
Scanning each other
Device collision interaction
Collaborative tasks

→ This makes the museum experience more dynamic and shared.

5. Immediate Feedback (Must-have)

The system must provide instant feedback (visual/audio/story updates) after user actions.
→ This reinforces engagement and keeps the experience responsive.
